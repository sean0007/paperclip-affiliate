TradingView Best Pick

★★★★★ — 4.9/5

What We Liked

  • Pine Script: Write your own indicators and strategies in Pine Script — the most powerful retail charting language available
  • Multi-chart layouts: Monitor up to 16 charts simultaneously on premium plans
  • Social charts: See what other traders are watching and copy their chart setups
  • Real-time data: Free tier has 5-second delays on crypto — not ideal but acceptable for non-day trading
  • Alert system: Best-in-class alert creation with complex conditional logic
  • Market scanner: Built-in stock/frypto/crypto screener with 100+ filters

✅ Pros

  • Best-in-class charts and drawing tools
  • Pine Script is genuinely powerful
  • Massive community of traders sharing ideas
  • Free tier is surprisingly complete
  • Works on web, desktop, and mobile

    • ❌ Cons

  • Data delays on free tier (5 seconds for crypto)
  • Pro+ ($15/mo) barely enough — Premium ($30/mo) is better value
  • Alert limits on lower tiers (1 alert on free)
  • No built-in exchange connectivity for automated trading

    • Best For

    99% of crypto traders. Whether you're a beginner learning technical analysis or an experienced trader running complex strategies, TradingView has everything you need.

    Verdict on Tiers

    Free: Fine for learning and casual charting. Limited to 1 indicator per chart, 1 alert, 3 charts in multi-chart view.
    Pro ($15/mo): Worth it. 3 alerts, 10 indicators per chart, unlimited chart layouts.
    Pro+ ($30/mo): Best value. 10 alerts, 25 indicators, 4 charts in multi-view, market scanner access.
    Premium ($60/mo): Only if you're running a trading business or need 1-second crypto data.

    TrendSpider

    ★★★★☆ — 4.4/5
    $35–$149/mo

    TrendSpider's main differentiator is automation. It can automatically detect trendlines, identify key levels, and run backtests — things that take hours manually but take TrendSpider seconds.

    What We Liked

    • Automated trendline detection: Draw trendlines automatically across multiple timeframes — genuinely impressive
    • Multi-timeframe analysis: See the same asset across daily, 4H, 1H, 15min in one view
    • Strategy backtesting: Test moving average crossover and other strategies directly on the chart
    • Dynamic alerts: Alerts tied to trendlines and patterns, not just price levels
    • Raindrop charts: Unique chart type that shows volume distribution at price levels

    ✅ Pros

  • Automated technical analysis saves hours
  • Best-in-class pattern recognition
  • Multi-timeframe analysis is genuinely useful
  • Strategy backtesting without coding

    • ❌ Cons

  • Expensive — cheapest plan is $35/mo
  • Crypto data is secondary to equities
  • Learning curve higher than TradingView
  • Pine Script not supported — you're limited to built-in tools

    • Verdict

    Worth it if you hate drawing trendlines manually and want automated technical analysis. If you're comfortable doing your own TA and prefer TradingView's ecosystem, save the money.

    Bookmap

    ★★★★☆ — 4.3/5
    $49–$149/mo

    Bookmap is for traders who want to see the order book in real-time. It's a specialized tool that shows liquidity and order flow — essential for understanding where big orders are sitting and where price is likely to go next.

    What We Liked

    • Order book heatmaps: Visual representation of buy/sell wall depth
    • Real-time liquidity tracking: See exactly where large orders are sitting
    • Volume tile charts: Unique visualization showing volume at each price level
    • DeFi support: Works with exchanges via API

    ✅ Pros

  • Best order book visualization available
  • Reveals liquidity traps before they trigger
  • Unique edge for scalpers and day traders

    • ❌ Cons

  • $49/mo minimum to start
  • No free tier — not even a trial
  • Crypto support limited vs. equities
  • Steep learning curve — takes weeks to master
  • Not a complete trading platform

    • Verdict

    Specialized tool for serious day traders. If you're scalping or doing high-frequency crypto trading, Bookmap is worth the investment. For everyone else, TradingView does the job.

    DexTools / DexScreener

    ★★★★☆ — 4.2/5
    Free

    For DeFi traders, DexTools and DexScreener are the standard. They show real-time swap data, liquidity, token trading activity, and price charts for any token on Ethereum, BSC, Polygon, Solana, and other chains.

    What We Liked

    • Free and instant: No account needed, works immediately
    • Real-time swap tracking: See every buy/sell as it happens
    • Token liquidity checks: Verify if a token has locked liquidity before buying
    • Multi-chain: Covers Ethereum, BSC, Solana, Polygon, Avalanche, and more
    • Pair explorer: Find new pairs before they pump

    ✅ Pros

  • Completely free — no paid tier needed
  • Essential for any DeFi trader
  • Real-time data, no delays
  • Covers every major DEX on every major chain

    • ❌ Cons

  • Basic charting — not a substitute for TradingView
  • Scam token noise — requires experience to filter
  • No built-in indicators or analysis tools
  • Can be overwhelming for beginners

    • Verdict

    Every DeFi trader needs DexTools open. It's free, fast, and gives you information that no other free tool provides. Use it alongside TradingView — not instead of it.

    Best Free Charting Options

    If you can't afford or don't want to pay for premium tools, here's what actually works for free:

    1. TradingView Free Tier (Recommended)

    Already covered above. The free tier is genuinely good enough for most traders. You get 3 indicators per chart, 1 alert, and 3 saved charts. The 5-second crypto delay is the main downside for day traders.

    2. Exchange Charts (Free)

    Binance, Bybit, and OKX all have built-in charting that's gotten significantly better. Binance's charting includes 100+ indicators and most standard drawing tools. Good enough for basic TA.

    3. Coinigy ($0–$99/mo)

    Connects to 20+ exchanges via API and provides unified charting. Free 30-day trial, then $99/mo for real-time data. Not worth it unless you're actively trading across 5+ exchanges simultaneously.

    4. Gooinv / TradingLite

    Free order book visualization tools popular among DeFi traders. TradingLite offers free orderflow charts on major DEX pairs. Niche but powerful for those who know what they're looking at.
