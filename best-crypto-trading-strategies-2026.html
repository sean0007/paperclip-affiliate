1. Trend Following

★★★★★ Tested on Freqtrade · Profit Factor: 1.10

The classic "the trend is your friend" strategy. Buy when price breaks above a moving average, sell when it breaks below. Works best in markets with clear directional moves.

📊 Our Backtest Results (BTC/USDT 4H, 90 Days)

Profit Factor1.10
Total Trades178
Win Rate52%
Max Drawdown18%
Best Timeframe4H or Daily
Best MarketTrending / Bull

How It Works

  • Buy when price crosses above 20 EMA (exponential moving average)
  • Sell when price crosses below 20 EMA
  • Can add RSI filter: only buy when RSI < 70 (not overbought)
  • Stop loss typically 2-3% below entry

Pros

  • Catches big trending moves — the biggest wins come from trends
  • Easy to automate with Freqtrade or exchange native bots
  • Works well on BTC, ETH, and high-cap alts
  • Clear entry and exit rules — no guesswork

Cons

  • Whipsaws in ranging markets — many small losses
  • Needs volatility to work — flat markets = losses
  • Needs proper position sizing — one bad trade can hurt

2. Mean Reversion

★★★☆☆ Tested on Freqtrade · Profit Factor: 0.42

Buy when price drops too far below its average. Sell when it bounces back up. The idea: price always returns to its mean. Works in sideways markets, but struggles badly in trending markets.

📊 Our Backtest Results (ETH/USDT 1H, 90 Days)

Profit Factor0.42 ❌
Total Trades41
Win Rate38%
Max Drawdown31%
Best Timeframe1H or 4H
Best MarketRanging / Sideways

⚠️ Warning from our testing

Mean reversion FAILED badly in our testing during a bull market. RSI-based approaches were the wrong tool when BTC was making new highs. We killed this strategy within the first week. Only use in a confirmed ranging market — and have an exit plan when trends resume.

How It Works

  • Buy when RSI drops below 30 (oversold)
  • Sell when RSI rises above 70 (overbought)
  • Bollinger Bands: buy when price touches lower band
  • Tight stop loss required — trends can continue

Pros

  • High win rate when market is truly ranging
  • Clear entry points with RSI/Bollinger
  • Good for markets that consolidate

Cons

  • CATASTROPHIC losses in strong trends — RSI can stay oversold for weeks
  • Needs accurate market regime detection
  • Hard to automate cleanly
💥 Best for Volatile Markets

3. Breakout Trading

★★★★☆ Tested Conceptually · Proven in volatile conditions

Buy when price breaks above a key resistance level. Sell when it breaks below support. The goal: catch the beginning of a big move before everyone else does.

How It Works

  • Identify key support and resistance levels
  • Buy when price breaks above resistance with volume
  • Sell when price breaks below support
  • Use ATR (Average True Range) for stop loss sizing

Best Indicators

  • Support/Resistance (manual or indicator-based)
  • Volume spike confirmation
  • Donchian Channels (automated support/resistance)
  • VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)

Pros

  • High reward-to-risk if you catch a real breakout
  • Works well in volatile crypto markets
  • Can be automated with Donchian Channel strategies

Cons

  • Many false breakouts — crypto whipsaws a lot
  • Needs volume confirmation or you'll get rekt
  • Requires good risk management

How to Run It

🔄 Easiest to Run

4. Grid Trading (Automation Native)

★★★★☆ Conceptually Tested · Popular on Binance/Pionex

Place multiple buy and sell orders at regular intervals above and below the current price. Every price oscillation earns a small profit. Best in sideways/ranging markets.

How It Works

  • Set an upper price limit and lower price limit
  • Bot places buy orders at regular intervals below current price
  • Bot places sell orders at regular intervals above current price
  • Every grid level filled = small profit
  • Stop loss below lower limit to cap downside

Best For

  • BTC when it's in a clear range (e.g., $60K-$70K for months)
  • ETH during consolidation phases
  • Quiet market conditions — not during big news events

Pros

  • Completely hands-off once set up
  • Every grid = guaranteed profit if price oscillates
  • Easy to set up on Binance Grid Bot or Pionex

Cons

  • Bleeds if price goes one direction without bouncing back
  • Needs wide enough range or you'll hit limits fast
  • Capital efficiency is low (money tied up across all grids)

How to Run It

Use Binance Grid Bot (built into your account) or Pionex Grid Bot (free). Both are native to the exchange — no Freqtrade needed. Set upper/lower limits 10-20% apart, 20-50 grids. Try Pionex free →

🕐 Best for Long-Term Investors

5. DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)

★★★★★ Simplest Strategy · Best Risk-Adjusted Returns

Buy a fixed dollar amount at regular intervals regardless of price. Removes emotion entirely. Works over long timeframes. Not a trading strategy — it's an accumulation strategy.

How It Works

  • Set a fixed amount: $10, $50, $100 per week or day
  • Buy BTC/ETH automatically via Binance Recurring Buy or Freqtrade DCA
  • Never time the market — buy regardless of price
  • Don't sell until you hit your target

Why It Works

  • Removes emotion completely — no FOMO, no panic
  • Smooths entry price over time
  • No analysis required — set and forget
  • Warren Buffett-style approach applied to crypto

Backtesting DCA on BTC (2018-2025)

  • Buying $100/week of BTC at any point in 2018-2020 → profitable by 2021
  • Buying $100/week through the 2022 crash → average entry ~$35K
  • Anyone who DCA'd through 2022-2024 is significantly in profit today

How to Run It

Set up recurring buy on Binance or Coinbase (free). Or use Freqtrade DCA bot. Open Binance account →

Which Strategy Should You Use?

It depends on your time horizon, risk tolerance, and market conditions:

  • Active trader, 4H+ timeframe: Trend Following — our top pick based on backtesting
  • Want to set and forget: DCA on BTC — best risk-adjusted over long periods
  • BTC in a known range: Grid Trading — easy and hands-off
  • Volatile altcoin trading: Breakout with tight stops — high risk, high reward
  • Short-term speculation: Avoid mean reversion in bull markets — we lost money on it
